Workshop Session 4

LLMs with Jupyter notebooks

Large language models (LLMs) are transforming the way we work with text data. This hands-on workshop introduces the basics of text-as-data, including word embeddings and zero-shot learning, and demonstrates how to deploy a free, open-access LLM using Hugging Face in a Jupyter Notebook. Participants will follow a guided example of sentiment analysis to explore how LLMs can be used for real-world tasks. After the workshop, you will receive a set of curated notebooks to further explore LLMs and their functionalities on your own.

Skill level: Intermediate

Presenter: Valerie Li

Skill level: Intermediate

This workshop introduces one impactful way to gain academic credit for your data: publishing a data paper. Data papers are peer-reviewed journal articles that describe a dataset in detailâ€”how it was collected, its structure, and its potential value for reuse. Data papers can be particularly helpful for researchers as they will: 1) add a peer-reviewed publication to your CV; 2) increase the visibility and usability of your dataset; and 3) provide a way for others to cite your data that will contribute to your citation count.
